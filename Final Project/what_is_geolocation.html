What is Geolocation?

Geolocation determines the real-world geographic location of an object. This is done by generating a set of geographic coordinates such a latitude and longitude through GPS and using the coordinates to determine a meaningful location. They are 2 types of Geolocation data being collected: Active User and Passive Server Based Look Up. Collecting these data types can enables cross-referencing to achieve the most accurate geolocation results.

Geolocation is not only determined by the GPS. There are many ways Geolocation can be identified. They can be identified by IP address, MAC address, RF systems EXIF data. The most precise way of finding geolocation is through GPS.

Most apps usually ask for your permission before they start collecting geolocation data. However this can depend on which type of phone you are using. For example, Iphone or Android. The permission you give can range from 1 day to forever or until the app is closed.
